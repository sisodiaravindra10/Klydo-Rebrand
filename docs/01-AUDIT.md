# 01 — Audit

> The Indian fashion app market is loud, fast, and aesthetically exhausted. Klydo's opening exists because nobody in this space has paired editorial taste with quick-commerce speed.

---

## 1. Klydo today

**Product.** A quick-commerce fashion app for Gen Z (18–32). Multi-brand, curated drops, 15-minute to 4-hour delivery in Bengaluru. Try-and-buy at the doorstep — pay only for what you keep. AI stylist baked in.

**Surfaces.**
- iOS + Android app (the real product)
- klydo.in (a splash page linking to app stores — barely qualifies as a landing page today)
- Instagram (@klydo.official — primary brand surface today)

**What's working.**
- Speed wedge is rare. Nobody else delivers fashion in 30 minutes.
- Try-before-pay removes the size-fit risk that kills 60%+ of fashion cart conversions in India.
- Curated drops play to Gen-Z impulse and FOMO (scroll → tap → wear tonight).
- Verified brand partners removes the counterfeit anxiety that haunts cheaper marketplaces.

**What's leaking value.**
- No web shopping. Discovery dies if a friend shares a product link to someone who doesn't have the app.
- Landing page does zero brand work. Currently a download wall.
- App icon and visual identity read generic SaaS, not fashion.
- Brand voice is undefined — Instagram swings between "off-duty model" and "Big Billion Day banner."
- No public design language across surfaces; every screenshot looks like a different product.

---

## 2. The competitive set

We are not building a Myntra clone with faster shipping. We are building a different product. Here is what the competitive shelf looks like, and what each player has trained the Indian user to feel.

| App | Position | Visual feel | Trained behavior |
|-----|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Myntra** | Mass-market category leader | Dense, banner-stacked, sale-screaming | "Coupon hunt. Compare. Wait 4 days." |
| **Ajio** | Reliance mid-premium | Cleaner than Myntra, still grid-heavy | "Premium-ish, but still feels like a catalog." |
| **Nykaa Fashion** | Beauty-adjacent premium | Editorial-lite, busy hero stack | "Curated, but the app forgets it was curated halfway down." |
| **Meesho** | Tier-2/3 value play | Reseller-driven, price-first | "Cheap. Buy three sizes, return two." |
| **Snitch** | Men's fast fashion, vertically integrated | Tight, bold, IG-native | "Fits the feed. Ships in a week." |
| **Bewakoof / Urbanic** | Gen-Z casual | Trend-led, modal-heavy | "Cute, but the app is exhausting." |
| **Tira** | Reliance premium beauty | Clean, editorial | "Beauty, not fashion — but this is the polish bar." |

**The shared pathology.** All seven cram the home screen with banners, deal stacks, category rails, brand stores, "trending now," "deal of the day," "for you," "back in stock," and four CTAs above the fold. They have trained the Indian shopper to **skim past UI as noise**. None of them feel like fashion. They feel like coupons.

---

## 3. The slop tax

Patterns we will not inherit, because they are slop dressed as conversion optimization:

- **Banner stacks.** Six horizontal carousels on the home screen, each fighting for attention. Users learn to ignore them.
- **Price-strikethrough theatre.** Fake MRPs inflated so the discount looks bigger. Erodes trust.
- **Countdown timers on everything.** Cheapens real urgency (which we actually have — 15-min delivery).
- **Pop-up modals on launch.** "Get ₹100 off!" before the user has seen a single product.
- **Bottom nav with 5 unrelated icons.** Wishlist, Cart, Categories, Profile, Bag — users get lost.
- **Emoji-heavy push notifications.** "🔥🔥 MEGA SALE 🔥🔥" — trains the user to mute.
- **Generic stock photography.** Lifestyle stock photos of "happy shoppers" — never actual product.
- **Pull-to-refresh that loads a new ad.** Punishes engagement.
- **Sale-banner above the brand mark.** No fashion house in history has done this. We are not them.

---

## 4. Klydo's wedge

Three things, in priority order:

1. **Speed is the brand.** Nobody else can deliver fashion in 30 minutes. This is the unfair advantage. Every surface must lean on it — the timer on the home screen, the ETA on every product card, the courier's face on the order screen.

2. **Try-before-pay is the verb.** "Order" is what Myntra does. "Try" is what Klydo does. Every action in the app should reinforce that buying is the last step, not the first.

3. **Curation as a personality, not a tab.** Drops are timed releases — today's 7pm drop, tomorrow's brunch drop. The app shouldn't have "Trending." It should have a *point of view*.

---

## 5. The opportunity gaps

Six places we win because the incumbents physically can't follow:

- **Editorial layout in a transactional app.** Generous whitespace, magazine-grade typography. They can't ship this without breaking their banner-ad model.
- **Web shopping that's actually good.** Most fashion apps treat web as an afterthought. We treat it as a first-class peer to the app.
- **A real brand system.** Not a logo + color — a voice, a photo direction, a motion language, a typographic identity.
- **Honest pricing.** No fake MRP. The price is the price. Trust compounds.
- **WhatsApp-native sharing.** Product links that render beautifully in WhatsApp. Group decision-making is how Indian Gen Z shops.
- **AI stylist that ships outfits, not advice.** "Wear this Friday" → tap → it arrives by 6:30pm.
