# Klydo — product context

**Register: product.** We're building app UI (mobile/web shopping). The landing page is the gateway; the core deliverable is the mobile and web shopping experience.

## Users

Gen Z urban Indians, 18–32. Phone-first. They shop on the train, in the auto-rickshaw, in bed, in line at the food stall. One hand, right thumb. Trained by Instagram, Reels, and TikTok scroll grammar. Not by Myntra catalog grammar.

Income bracket: working-age young adults with disposable income. Concerned with size-fit (60% of fashion returns in India are sizing). Trust-skeptical (burned by fake-MRP marketplace tactics). Time-sensitive (want to wear it tonight, not next week).

## Product purpose

Klydo is the fashion app for the impatient with taste. Drops at 7pm. Delivered in 30 minutes. Try at the door. Pay only for what you keep. Bengaluru only at launch (Mumbai, Delhi, Hyderabad next).

Three things and nothing more:
1. Speed (30-min delivery, the unfair advantage)
2. Try-before-pay (the size-fit risk eliminator)
3. Curation (7pm drops with a point of view, not endless catalog)

## Brand

- **Voice:** friend who works in fashion, not a brand that sells fashion. Short. Lowercase for hero copy and section heads. Sentence case for body. Telegraphic. ("drops at 7. delivered by 7:30. try, keep, return.")
- **Verb:** *try.* This is the hero verb across every screen. "Try it on" beats "Add to bag."
- **Vibe:** loud, color-blocked, Gen-Z native, graphic-poster taste. Not editorial-Vogue (rejected in iteration 1). Closer to TikTok / Snitch / Coperni than to *Vogue India* or Aesop.

## Strategic principles

Full text in `docs/02-PRINCIPLES.md`. Ranked (lower-numbered wins when in conflict).

1. **Feed-native, not grid-native.** Vertical scroll, full-bleed, one product at a time.
2. **Graphic-poster taste, quick-commerce speed.** Chunky bold sans, color-block sections, marquees, lowercase voice. Paired with sub-300ms responsiveness.
3. **Speed is the brand. Surface it on every screen.** ETA on every card. Live countdown on drops. Driver's face on tracking.
4. **Try-before-pay is the hero verb, not a feature toggle.** Primary CTA on every product is "try."
5. **One thumb. Everything within reach.** Primary actions bottom-third, 48px tap targets.
6. **India built in, not retrofitted.** COD on par with UPI. WhatsApp share. ₹ Indian grouping. Local addressing ("opposite the Domino's").
7. **Motion must mean something.** Marquees signal "live." No decorative confetti.
8. **Restraint over feature creep.** Visually loud, featurally lean. One job per surface.
9. **Show the product, not the chrome.** Photography fills 80%+ of product pages. UI recedes.
10. **The price is the price.** No fake MRP. Real numbers, honest discounts.

## Anti-references (what we are not)

- **Myntra / Ajio / Nykaa Fashion / Meesho.** Banner stacks, coupon theatre, fake MRPs, dense catalog grids. They have trained the Indian shopper to skim past UI as noise.
- **Vogue print magazine layout.** Editorial restraint with serif display is too quiet for this audience (we tried it; rejected).
- **Generic AI-startup landing:** purple gradients, glassmorphism on everything, Inter on every headline, three-equal-feature-cards row.
- **Fashion-app tropes:** "Discover" as a verb. Stock photography of "happy shoppers." Hero countdowns on everything. Live-shopping video. Spin-the-wheel games.
